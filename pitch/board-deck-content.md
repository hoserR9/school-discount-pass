### Slide 2: The Problem & The Solution
**Current**: Physical cards cost money to print, get lost, can't track usage, and can't be updated once printed.

**Proposed**: A digital version that lives in Apple Wallet on every fan's phone — zero printing costs, built-in fraud prevention, real-time tracking, and the ability to push new deals to cardholders throughout the season.

Physical cards are still available for those who want them. Each physical card has a unique QR code that links it to a digital version.

---

### Slide 3: Revenue & Costs

**Revenue Model:**
- Physical card sales: $20 each (same as current)
- Sponsorship tiers: Gold ($500), Silver ($250), Bronze ($100)
- Projected: 200 cards x $20 = $4,000 + sponsorship revenue

**Costs:**
- Apple Developer Account: $99/year (one-time annual)
- Server hosting: ~$5-10/month (basic cloud hosting)
- Printing (physical cards with QR): existing budget
- No additional software licenses needed

**Net savings**: Eliminates reprinting costs when sponsors change. Digital distribution is free.

---

### Slide 4: Fraud Prevention & Security
Board concern: "What prevents screenshots/sharing?"

- Each card has a **unique ID** tied to the holder's name
- Verification screen shows the **cardholder's name** — cashiers can verify identity
- **Rate limiting**: flags if a card is scanned more than 5 times in 24 hours
- **Rapid scan detection**: warns cashier if same card was just used minutes ago at a different location
- Suspicious cards turn **yellow warning** instead of green
- Admin can **deactivate** any card instantly from the dashboard

---

### Slide 5: What Sponsors See
Board concern: "How do sponsors access their data?"

Each sponsor gets a **private dashboard** at their own login page:
- Total visits from cardholders
- Unique customers
- Visits broken down by day
- Recent scan history with dates
- Access via a simple code — no app needed, works on any phone or computer

This gives sponsors clear ROI data, which makes them more likely to renew next year.

---

### Slide 6: How Promotions Work
Board concern: "Can we push updates to cardholders?"

Yes. The admin dashboard lets you:
1. Create a promotion (e.g., "Game Day: 20% off at Board & Brew tonight")
2. Push it to every cardholder's phone with one click
3. Their Apple Wallet card updates automatically with a lock-screen notification
4. Promotion can have an expiration date — it disappears when it ends

Sponsors can request promotions tied to game days, homecoming, senior night, etc. This drives foot traffic and keeps the program active all season — not just at card purchase time.
